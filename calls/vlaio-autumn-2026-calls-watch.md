## Description

VLAIO runs an annual call cycle; all 2025-2026 round deadlines have passed (February–March 2026). The next wave opens in autumn 2026 for the 2026-2027 cycle. Key instruments relevant to the profile:

| Instrument | Focus | Typical budget | Expected opening |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LA-trajecten** (landbouw) | Demand-driven applied research for agriculture & horticulture sector | €200k–€1M | Sep–Oct 2026 |
| **TETRA** (with AI focus) | Translating academic knowledge to SMEs; AI track (TETRA VAIOP) active | €150k–€500k | Oct–Nov 2026 |
| **COOCK+** (with AI focus) | Collective R&D and knowledge dissemination; AI track (COOCK+ VAIOP) active | €300k–€1M | Oct–Nov 2026 |

| **Innovation Mandates 2026B** | Post-doc knowledge transfer / spin-off | ~€150k per mandate | Sep 2026 |

## Relevance to Profile

- **Topics:** LA-trajecten directly covers agriculture, horticulture, biological control, food safety/security; TETRA/COOCK+ cover AI transfer to agri-food SMEs
- **Keywords:** TETRA and COOCK+ have an explicit AI priority track (VAIOP) since 2025-2026 that continues into 2026-2027
- **Institution type:** research institutes (hogescholen, universities, research centres) are the primary eligible applicants for TETRA, COOCK+, and LA-trajecten; Baekeland/Innovation Mandates require industry partnership
- **Consortium size:** TETRA/COOCK+ require a user committee of Flemish companies (5–15 SMEs typical); LA-trajecten similar; fits small-to-large consortium model
- **Deadline constraint:** expected deadlines are autumn 2026 (Sep–Nov), well beyond the 3-month constraint; monitor VLAIO news feed from August 2026 for official announcements
- **Priority theme for 2025-2026 LA-trajecten was sustainable soil management** (€5M reserved); 2026-2027 priority theme not yet announced — likely announced at opening of the call
- **Action to take:** monitor vlaio.be/nl/nieuws from August 2026; prepare project concept notes now, particularly for LA-trajecten (agriculture/biological control) and TETRA/COOCK+ VAIOP (AI transfer to agri-food SMEs)
