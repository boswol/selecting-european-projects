## Description

LIFE Standard Action Projects (SAPs) for Nature & Biodiversity support concrete on-the-ground conservation and restoration activities aimed at halting and reversing biodiversity loss across European habitats and species. The 2026 calls were published on 21 April 2026; CINEA hosted online info days 28–30 April 2026. Projects typically run 4–6 years with at least one partner from the private sector or civil society.

## Relevance to Profile

- **Topics:** biological control (ecosystem services, natural pest regulation, habitat connectivity) — indirect match; agriculture and food safety are not primary themes
- **Keywords:** AI tools are not a stated priority; remote sensing and monitoring data use is encouraged but not required
- **Institution type:** research institutes are eligible, usually as scientific/technical partners alongside NGOs or public authorities
- **Consortium size:** no strict minimum, but multi-partner projects are preferred; small (3–5) works well
- **Deadline constraint:** September 22, 2026 is 5 months away — fully within range; call just published
- **Note:** better fit for biological control in agro-ecosystem contexts than for food safety or AI; worth considering as a secondary opportunity if a strong biodiversity/biocontrol angle can be framed
