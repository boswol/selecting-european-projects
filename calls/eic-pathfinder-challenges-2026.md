# EIC Pathfinder Challenges 2026

- **Programme:** European Innovation Council (EIC) — Pathfinder Challenges
- **Call ID:** EIC-PATHFINDER-CHALLENGES-2026
- **URL:** https://eic.ec.europa.eu/eic-funding-opportunities/eic-pathfinder/eic-pathfinder-challenges-2026_en
- **Deadline:** 28 October 2026, 17:00 CEST
- **Budget per project:** up to €4M (€32M per challenge, €96M total across 3 challenges)
- **Action type:** Pathfinder (visionary, high-risk exploratory research, TRL 1–4)
- **Fit score:** 2/5 — budget and consortium size match well; one AI challenge (DeepRAP) is thematically adjacent; however none of the 3 defined challenges directly targets food, agriculture, or biological control

## Description

The 2026 EIC Pathfinder Challenges fund visionary, high-risk research aimed at future breakthrough technologies. The three defined challenges for 2026 are: (1) Advanced Materials for Miniaturised Energy Harvesting Systems, (2) Biotechnology for Healthy Ageing, and (3) DeepRAP — Deep Reasoning, Abstraction & Planning towards trustworthy Cognitive AI Systems. Each challenge has a budget of €32M and expects to fund approximately 8–10 projects at up to €4M each. Requires min 3 partners from ≥3 countries (at least one EU Member State).

## Relevance to Profile

- **Topics:** none of the 3 challenges directly covers food safety, food security, agriculture, or biological control; DeepRAP covers AI reasoning which could be stretched to agricultural decision-support contexts
- **Keywords:** AI and agentic AI are central to DeepRAP, but the challenge scope is cognitive/general AI — not applied to food or agri domains
- **Institution type:** research institutes are fully eligible and typical applicants
- **Consortium size:** min 3 from 3 countries — fits small consortium preference (3–5)
- **Deadline constraint:** October 28, 2026 is 6 months away — well within range
- **Note:** monitor EIC Pathfinder Challenges 2027 for a possible agriculture/food biotechnology or AI-in-food-safety challenge; the 2025 programme included a biotech-for-soil-regeneration challenge which would have been a stronger fit
